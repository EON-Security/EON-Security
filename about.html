About EON Security

Built on Experience. Driven by Results.

The EON Security Story

EON Security was founded with a singular mission: to provide genuine, proactive security support to organizations that need it most. What started as CyberAdvent in 2022 has evolved into EON Security—a commitment to serving local businesses and government organizations with real-world offensive security expertise.

The catalyst was witnessing firsthand the devastating impact of a ransomware attack on an organization. That moment crystallized a simple truth: reactive security isn't enough. Businesses need partners who can identify and close security gaps before adversaries exploit them. Since then, EON Security has been dedicated to preventing those attacks through proactive security measures and real adversary emulation.

With nearly 4 years of focused offensive security experience and over 5 years supporting government organizations, EON Security brings a unique perspective to the table. This isn't just another penetration testing shop running automated scans—this is security testing informed by years of hands-on experience across the entire technology stack.

A Foundation Built on Understanding

What sets EON Security apart is a deep understanding of not just how to find vulnerabilities, but why they exist and how to fix them effectively. This perspective comes from a diverse background that spans the entire IT spectrum—from helpdesk analyst and system administrator roles to threat hunting, penetration testing, and red team operations.

That system administrator experience? It's invaluable. When EON Security identifies a security issue, it's not just flagged as "bad"—there's an understanding of why an administrator might have configured something a particular way, the operational constraints they face, and most importantly, how to remediate the issue without breaking business operations. This empathy for the defender's perspective makes EON Security's recommendations practical, not just technically sound.

The progression from defense to offense—moving through threat hunting, penetration testing, and red team operations—provides the adversarial mindset needed to truly test an organization's security posture. But it's always grounded in the reality of what defenders actually face.
